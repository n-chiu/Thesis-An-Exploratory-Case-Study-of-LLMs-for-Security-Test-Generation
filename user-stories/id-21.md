# (21) Insecure Design + Mishandling of Exceptional Conditions

## Title: Realistic business logic flow

## ID: 21

## User Story:
As a Software company Customer, I want the application to use business logic with a sequentially ordered flow that includes limits to prevent automated attacks against financial modules and PII.

## Acceptance Criteria:

Validate the application will only process business logic flows in sequential step order, with all steps being processed in realistic human time, and not process out of order, skipped steps, process steps from another user, or too quickly submitted transactions.

Ensure the application has business limits and correctly enforces on a per user basis, with configurable alerting and automated reactions to automated or unusual attack.

## Edge Cases:

Users bypassing steps via direct API calls.

Replay attacks on transaction steps.

Nots executing flows faster than humanly possible.

Multi-tab/session confusing causing out-of-order execution.

Race conditions in financial transactions.

Users manipulating client-side validation.

Legitimate high-speed users flagged incorrectly.

## Constraints:

Server-side enforcement of step sequencing (never trust client).

Use anti-automation controls (CAPTCHA, rate limiting, behavioural analysis).

Enforce minimum/maximum time thresholds between steps where applicable.

Each transaction must be bound to a user/session context.

Idempotency and replay protection required.

Configurable transaction limits per user.

Monitoring and alerting for abnormal patterns.

Strong consistency controls for critical operation.

## Vulnerability Category:

Insecure Design + Mishandling of Exceptional Conditions

Insecure Design: 841, 799.
